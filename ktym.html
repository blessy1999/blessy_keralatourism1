KOTTAYAM

Kottayam is a city in the Indian state of Kerala. Kottayam literally means the interior of a fort—Kotta + Akam. Flanked by the Western Ghats on the east and the Vembanad Lake and paddy fields of Kuttanad on the west, Kottayam is a place that is known for extraordinary qualities.

ILLIKAL KALLU

Illikkal Kallu is a monolith located on top of the Illickal Malaa in the Kottayam district of Kerala, India.The distance from kottayam railway station to illikal kallu is 57km.

ILAVEEZHAPOONCHIRA

Ilaveezhapoonchira is a tourist destination located in Melukavu village in Kottayam district near Kanjar. Ilaveezha Poonchira is surrounded by three enchanting hillocks - Mankunnu, Kodayathoormala and Thonippara. This makes the place ideal for trekking.

KUMARAKAM

At a distance of 16 km from Kottayam, 56 km from Kochi, 36 km from Alleppey, 135 km from Munnar & 164 km from Trivandrum, Kumarakom is a cluster of little islands on the Vembanad Lake in Kerala. Also known as the Queen of Vembanad, Kumarakom is the most famous Kerala backwaters destination for traditional houseboat ride and it is among the most visited Tourist places in kerala state.

POONJAR PALACE

Poonjar Palace, built in the Meenachil taluk is a stunning heritage site worth a visit. It is just 30 kilometers from Kottayam railway station, and sports a temple that is a true replica of the Madurai Meenakshi Temple. The palace is famous for its rich collection of antiques, chandeliers, jewel boxes, grain measure, weaponry, palm leaf engravings, statues, sculptures of Nataraja and palanquin.

TAJ JUMA MASJID

Taj Juma Masjid, as this mosque is also known, is built on the banks of Meenachil River. The mosque is noted for its magnificent wood carvings and spectacular pillars. Built by the Muslim settlers of Kerala, the mosque is located at a distance of about 5 kilometers from Baker’s Junction, one of another famous landmarks of Kerala.
